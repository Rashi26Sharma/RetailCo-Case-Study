🚀 Project Title: Inclusive Supply Chain Strategy for RetailCo

📌 Description

RetailCo, a large global retailer, recognized a significant gap in its supplier base: Minority- and Women-Owned Business Enterprises (MWBEs) were severely underrepresented despite being qualified, product-ready businesses. The issue wasn't capability  it was access. RetailCo's procurement system had been built to optimize inventory and minimize risk at scale, which worked well for large established suppliers but systematically shut out smaller MWBE businesses through rigid minimum order requirements, network-driven sourcing, and no active outreach.

Two concrete strategic goals were set to address this — double the number of MWBE suppliers in the ecosystem, and deploy a $25 million investment fund through a Community Development Financial Institution (CDFI) to give underserved MWBEs the capital they needed to compete.

This project presents a comprehensive, data-driven strategy to build an inclusive and sustainable supply chain for a global retailer (RetailCo), with a strong focus on increasing participation from Minority- and Women-Owned Business Enterprises (MWBEs).

It combines strategic thinking, data analysis, and real-world business problem-solving to address key barriers such as limited supplier access, financing constraints, procurement rigidity, and systemic bias.

The Core Insight

The most important finding wasn't a number   it was a reframe. RetailCo's MWBE problem is not a quality problem and it is not a risk problem. It is a design problem. The systems, standards, and behaviors that govern how suppliers are found, evaluated, and onboarded were never built with MWBE access in mind. Fixing it doesn't require lowering the bar — it requires redesigning the gate.

# RetailCo-Case-Study

RetailCo: Inclusive Supply Chain 
The Problem

RetailCo, a large global retailer, recognized a significant gap in its supplier base: Minority- and Women-Owned Business Enterprises (MWBEs) were severely underrepresented despite being qualified, product-ready businesses. The issue wasn't capability  it was access. RetailCo's procurement system had been built to optimize inventory and minimize risk at scale, which worked well for large established suppliers but systematically shut out smaller MWBE businesses through rigid minimum order requirements, network-driven sourcing, and no active outreach.
Two concrete strategic goals were set to address this — double the number of MWBE suppliers in the ecosystem, and deploy a $25 million investment fund through a Community Development Financial Institution (CDFI) to give underserved MWBEs the capital they needed to compete.

What I Did

The engagement was structured as a full consulting simulation across two parallel workstreams, delivered over a 10-week timeline.

Task 1 — Project Planning. I designed the complete 10-week project plan covering both workstreams from scratch. This meant breaking down each workstream into phases (Research, Fact-base, Strategy Development, Roll-out for the MWBE Supplier % stream , Research, Investment Thesis, Fund Options, Operating Model, Shortlist, and Selection for the CDFI stream), identifying every key activity within each phase, mapping dependencies, and structuring the timeline so both workstreams could run in parallel and converge at a mid-point readout in Week 5.

Task 2 — Research, Insights & Strategy. I synthesized qualitative and quantitative data from two sources. On the qualitative side, I analyzed notes from 9 stakeholder interviews — 5 procurement executives (including RetailCo's own CPO and Director of Procurement, plus three peer retailer benchmarks) and 4 MWBE business owners across clothing, candles, soft drinks, and party goods. On the quantitative side, I analyzed Likert-scale survey data from procurement professionals and MWBE owners across statements covering supplier tracking, financing programs, bias, networking, and the perceived impact of diversity. From this I identified four core barriers — network and access constraints, financing and capital gaps, rigid procurement standards, and unconscious bias — and developed seven strategic recommendations organized under three pillars: Network & Outreach, Financing & Capital, and Procurement Reform.

The Core Insight

The most important finding wasn't a number   it was a reframe. RetailCo's MWBE problem is not a quality problem and it is not a risk problem. It is a design problem. The systems, standards, and behaviors that govern how suppliers are found, evaluated, and onboarded were never built with MWBE access in mind. Fixing it doesn't require lowering the bar — it requires redesigning the gate.
